Keplerian Elements

In order to uniquely define an orbit, you need at least six parameters often called orbital elements.

The most common set of orbital elements is called the Keplerian Elements, named after Johannes Kepler. They are idealized, mathematical approximations of an orbit, ignoring a real orbit's tend to change over time due to gravitational perturbations by other objects.

Two elements describe the shape of the orbit, eccentricity and the semimajor axis. The inclination, argument of perigee, and right ascension of the ascending node describe the orientation of the orbit. Lastly, the true anomaly describes the object's position in the orbit.
